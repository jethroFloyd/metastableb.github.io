Don Dennis

Research Fellow @ Microsoft Research India

Hi there

I am a Research Fellow in the Machine Learning and Optimization (MLO) group and the Intelligent Devices Expedition (IDE) at Microsoft Research India working on bringing machine learning and intelligence to tiny, edge and end point devices - think devices with few KBs of RAM and bare minimum processing capabilities. I am advised by Prateek and Harsha.

My research focuses on developing and analysing machine learning algorithms that ensures state-of-the art performance in resource constrained environments. I am also interested in understanding the systems aspects of such environments, how ML algorithms and the devices that they work on interact and how such interactions can be made efficient. The GesturePod and WakeWord projects that I am working on currently are specific examples of how novel machine learning ideas with efficient on device implementations can give rise to amazing practical and durable intelligent devices. The EMI-RNN algorithm (accepted at NIPS '18) we recently developed is an example of a machine learning technique that shines in the resource constrained setting.

I one of the first and a core contributor of EdgeML - our machine learning library for edge devices. Along with Aditya, I develop and maintain our Tensorflow code base.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, and its applications and interactions with systems and devices. Broadly, I am interested in the theory and application of ML algorithms that can solve real world problems. I am also interested in getting such algorithms to work on interesting devices.
